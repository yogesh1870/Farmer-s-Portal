Government Policies for Farmers

Discover the policies implemented by the government to support and empower farmers.

Policy 1: Agricultural Subsidy Scheme - A scheme providing subsidies on farming equipment and seeds to boost agricultural productivity.

Policy 2: Crop Insurance Scheme - Government-backed crop insurance schemes to help farmers recover from unexpected crop failures due to natural disasters.

Policy 3: Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) - A crop insurance scheme to reduce the financial burden on farmers from natural calamities, pests, and diseases.

Policy 4: Soil Health Management - A policy promoting soil testing, educating farmers on sustainable farming practices, and ensuring better crop yield.

Policy 5: National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture - Focuses on promoting sustainable farming practices and conserving natural resources for long-term productivity.

Policy 6: National Agricultural Policy - Aimed at achieving higher growth and sustainability in agriculture while improving farmers' livelihoods.

Policy 7: Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) - A scheme to promote growth in the agricultural sector by providing financial support to various agricultural development projects.

Policy 8: Atma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyan - A policy aimed at promoting self-reliance in agriculture and rural economy by encouraging local agricultural products and innovations.

Policy 9: PM-GKAY (Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana) - A policy to provide free food grains to farmers and their families during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Policy 10: Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) - Aims to provide irrigation facilities to farmers and ensure the optimal use of water resources.

Farmer Schemes

Government schemes that provide financial and technical support for farmers across India.

Scheme 1: PM-Kisan Scheme - Direct income support of ₹6,000 per year to eligible farmers.

Scheme 2: Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) - Aims to provide irrigation facilities to farmers and ensure the optimal use of water resources.

Scheme 3: Kisan Credit Card (KCC) - Provides short-term credit to farmers for their agricultural needs at affordable interest rates.

Scheme 4: Agricultural Market Reforms - Strengthening of agricultural markets and providing access to e-marketplaces for farmers to sell their produce directly.

Scheme 5: Soil Health Management - A program to help farmers adopt sustainable practices and improve soil fertility for increased agricultural productivity.

Scheme 6: National Agricultural Market (eNAM) - A scheme that links agricultural markets across India to enhance market accessibility and better price realization for farmers.

Scheme 7: PM-FME (Prime Minister Formalization of Micro Food Enterprises) - Supports farmers, food processors, and rural entrepreneurs to scale up their businesses.

Scheme 8: Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) - A crop insurance scheme to safeguard farmers against natural disasters and crop failures.

Scheme 9: National Food Security Mission (NFSM) - A scheme to increase food production and improve farm income through the promotion of technology, seeds, and equipment.

Scheme 10: Kisan Rail Yojana - A scheme aimed at transporting perishable farm produce to distant markets to reduce wastage and increase farmers’ income.

Scheme 11: Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF) - Aimed at developing rural infrastructure and enhancing farmers' access to resources.

Scheme 12: Sustainable Agriculture Development - Promotes sustainable practices in farming, focusing on organic farming and climate-resilient crops.

Scheme 13: Digital Agriculture - Encourages the use of technology and digital tools for efficient farming practices and better crop management.

Scheme 14: Organic Farming Scheme - A scheme to promote organic farming and help farmers switch to eco-friendly farming methods.

Scheme 15: Agricultural Infrastructure Fund (AIF) - Provides funding for developing infrastructure such as cold storage, warehousing, and processing units to improve agricultural supply chains.

Government Policies and Schemes for Farmers by Bank

State Bank of India (SBI)

Loan Scheme: SBI offers special agricultural loans with low-interest rates under government schemes like Kisan Credit Cards (KCC) and NABARD support.

Policy Support: SBI supports financial assistance to farmers for crop insurance, farm mechanization, and agricultural development projects under government schemes.

Additional Loan Offer: SBI provides loans under PM-Kisan Scheme and PM-FME for farmers and small food processors.

Punjab National Bank (PNB)

Loan Scheme: PNB offers affordable loans for agricultural development and farm equipment purchases under government schemes like PM-Kisan and NABARD funding.

Policy Support: The bank supports government schemes for agricultural development, crop insurance, and farm mechanization.

Additional Loan Offer: PNB also provides loans for purchasing seeds, fertilizers, and irrigation systems to enhance productivity.

Bank of India (BOI)

Loan Scheme: BOI provides specialized loans for farmers under government-backed programs such as PM-FME and NABARD funding.

Policy Support: The bank assists farmers in accessing schemes like Kisan Credit Cards and agricultural insurance policies, making farming more financially secure.

Additional Loan Offer: BOI also offers long-term loans for the development of agriculture infrastructure and equipment to improve efficiency.

ICICI Bank

Loan Scheme: ICICI Bank provides agriculture loan schemes like Kisan Credit Card and short-term loans to meet the farming requirements for seasonal crops.

Policy Support: ICICI Bank offers loans under PMFBY to insure crops and provide financial relief to farmers.

Additional Loan Offer: The bank also supports rural farmers with special financing for infrastructure development, including irrigation systems and cold storage units.

HDFC Bank

Loan Scheme: HDFC offers financial assistance to farmers for crop production, purchase of agricultural machinery, and farm mechanization under the Kisan Credit Card Scheme.

Policy Support: The bank offers easy access to government schemes like PM-Kisan and PMFBY, supporting farmers in times of crisis.

Additional Loan Offer: HDFC Bank supports farmers with long-term loans for setting up greenhouses, irrigation systems, and renewable energy sources for sustainable farming.
